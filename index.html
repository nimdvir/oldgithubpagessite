ABOUT

Nim Dvir is a PhD student in the department of Informatics and Information Science, specializing in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Knowledge Organization and Management (KOM).


His research interests revolve around user experience (UX), social computing, cyber behavior, usability studies, content strategy, interaction design, and the application of information and communication technologies (ICTs) toward social, economic, and political development.


Prior to his doctoral studies, Nim worked for over 12 years as a UX and marketing researcher, product manager, digital content strategist and journalist for various media companies and non-profit organizations in New York City and Tel-Aviv.


Nim holds an M.B.A. from Baruch College (City University of New York - CUNY) and a B.A. from New York University (NYU), both with honors. He is American, German (EU) and Israeli citizen.
